Loan Amortization Calculator - User Guide

The Loan Amortization Calculator helps users understand loan repayment structures, interest calculations, and financial implications. By entering loan details, users can analyze different repayment scenarios and their impact on total interest.

1. Loan Type

The calculator supports four types of loans:

  • Amortized Loan (Reducing Balance): Regular payments consisting of principal and interest. The principal portion increases over time while interest decreases, ensuring full repayment.
  • Interest-Only Loan: Only interest payments are made during a specified period. The full principal is due at the end of the term.
  • Partially Amortized Loan: Regular payments reduce the principal, but a portion remains unpaid. This remaining balance (balloon payment) is due at the end of the loan.
    • Note: For partially amortized loans, if the loan is partially amortized, the balloon payment must be specified; otherwise, it is defaulted to zero.
  • Flat Interest Rate Loan: This loan type has constant interest and principal repayments. The interest is calculated as a percentage of the amount borrowed, divided by the loan duration, while the capital repayment is the amount borrowed divided by the loan duration.
    • Note: For flat interest rate loans, the repayment description is set to "Level" by default, and the interest type is set to "Nominal" by default. These default values are not influenced by their corresponding input cells.

2. Repayment Timeframe

Determines how often loan payments are made per year:

  • Annually (once per year)
  • Semi-Annually (twice per year)
  • Quarterly (four times per year)
  • Bimonthly (six times per year)
  • Monthly (twelve times per year - default)

3. Interest Rate

The cost of borrowing, expressed as a percentage:

  • Effective Interest Rate (default): Adjusted for compounding periods.
  • Nominal Interest Rate: The stated rate, not adjusted for compounding.

4. Repayment Description

Determines how the loan principal is repaid:

  • Level (Default): Fixed payments throughout the loan term.
  • Increasing: Starts with a lower payment that gradually increases.
  • Decreasing: Starts with a higher payment that gradually decreases.
Note: For increasing or decreasing repayments, specify the adjustment amount using the "By" input field; otherwise, it defaults to zero. Flat interest rate loans do not require this field.

5. Amount Borrowed

The total loan amount requested, which serves as the principal for interest calculations.

6. Loan Term

The duration of the loan, which represents how long it will last. It is measured in months.

7. Start Date

The date the loan is initiated, used to schedule repayments and calculate interest accrual.

8. Client Name

A field for businesses or individuals to personalize their loan schedule report. The entered name appears on the printed report.

Conclusion

Understanding loan inputs is crucial for financial planning. This calculator helps borrowers and lenders compare different loan structures and repayment plans to make informed financial decisions.
